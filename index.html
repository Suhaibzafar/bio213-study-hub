Straight from your professor — know these cold

  • Hydrogen bonds hold the two strands of DNA together.
  • DNA strands are read 5′ → 3′ (start to end, left to right).
  • Two processes: DNA → RNA = transcription; RNA → protein = translation.
  • RNA types to know: hnRNA, mRNA, rRNA, tRNA.
  • DNA vs. RNA: RNA gets a 5′ cap and a poly-A tail; introns are removed by splicing, done by spliceosomes.
  • The longest part of the cell cycle is interphase. Cells in G₀ never divide — but they are not dead.
  • Sister chromatids separate in anaphase of mitosis and anaphase II of meiosis.

These exact points are woven into the notes, quizzes, and flashcards below.
